Full Day Kindergarten Builds Social Emotional Competence

  • January 23, 2015
  • Jane Lindsey, Clark School Guidance Counselor

Social Emotional Competence sets the foundation for learning and school success and can be as good if not a better predictor of school success than cognitive ability or academic preparedness. Early intervention at a time when childre's behavior is the most malleable is the most beneficial means of preventing escalating academic problems including grade retention, school drop-out and anti-social behavior. Children are not available for learning if they do not have the skills to self-regulate and to manage behaviors or intense feelings. Think about yourselves when you are struggling with a personal issue; you have a harder time concentrating on other things in your life.

A full day kindergarten program would give all students the much needed opportunity to develop the skills to self-regulate, to focus their attention and to be available for learning as well as to develop the skills to problem solve and navigate their social world. In this day and age, our children are involved in so many adult driven activities vs. child initiated and we are seeing children who increasingly lack the skills to manage and resolve conflicts or to cope with strong emotions. A full day program would afford all students more opportunities to work on these critical skills and would set the stage for school success.

With the rigors of the common core curriculum which is intended to be delivered in a full day program but is being implemented in 2 and 3/4 hours, children are not given the benefit of time to work on social learning and learning through play which is critical at this young age. The children certainly appear to be feeling the pressure which can only lead to an increase in the amount of stress that these children are feeling. In addition so many of our parents don't have the luxury of staying home with their children therefore, many kids are going to two and three different programs in a given day which can be very stressful and overwhelming.

As the Clark School Guidance Counselor I urge you to consider what is in the best interest of our children.
